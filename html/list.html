3. Unordered Lists (<ul>)

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Bread
  • Hummus

Ideal for grocery lists or feature lists where sequence is unimportant.

4. Ordered Lists (<ol>)

  1. Drive to the end of the road
  2. Turn right
  3. Go straight through two roundabouts
  4. Turn left at the third roundabout
  5. School is on your right

Great for step-by-step instructions or ranked lists.

5. Nesting Lists

  1. Process all ingredients into a paste.
    • For chunky hummus, blend briefly.
    • For smooth hummus, blend longer.
  2. Season with salt, garlic, and lemon juice.
  3. Drizzle olive oil on top and serve.

Combine <ul> or <ol> inside <li> for sub-items.

6. Description Lists (<dl>)

Soliloquy
A speech in which a character speaks thoughts aloud to the audience.
Monologue
A long speech spoken by a character to other characters or audience.
Aside
A remark intended only for the audience, unheard by other characters.

Perfect for glossaries, FAQs, or any term–definition content.
